Grade-by-Grade Signal Protocols

Position Size100–125% of standard lot. Justify the 25% premium: Grade 5 has 97–100% historical WR across 94 trades. Maximum capital deployment is correct here.

Section 4

Active Trade Management

⚡ The 2R Partial Exit — Non-Negotiable

When price reaches Entry + 2 × (Entry − Stop): sell exactly 50% of the position and move the stop on the remaining 50% to the entry price (breakeven).

This single action converts a "possible winner" into a guaranteed winner. The first 50% locks in 2R profit. The remaining 50% has zero downside — it can only produce breakeven or better.

Missed 2R partials are the most common cause of underperformance vs backtest. After executing, update the Live Grade column to "→★5 (2R hit [date/time])". Target: 100% execution rate.

4.2 Grid Level Management

LevelWhen It FillsImmediate ActionsWhat Changed
L1 At entry Set hard stop. Set TP. Place L2 limit at Entry − grid spacing. Place L3 at Entry − 2× spacing. Position open. Standard management.
L2 Price moves adverse by grid spacing Calculate blended average. Adjust TP to blend ± 2.5R. Stop stays at original L1 hard stop — do NOT widen. EV of combined position improves. Better average entry. Grid working correctly.
L3 Price moves 2× adverse Recalculate 3-level blend. Update TP to blend ± 2.5R. Stop still at original L1 distance. Monitor closely. Maximum position. Highest raw EV (+108p avg). Most variance. The trade must be allowed to work without interference.

4.3 Stop Management Rules

Never move a stop adversely. Once placed, the hard stop is fixed until the 2R partial fires.
After 2R partial fires: move stop to entry price (breakeven). This is mandatory and immediate.
After partial, the stop may be trailed at discretion — but never moved adversely. Options: keep at breakeven (safest) or trail at 0.5× ATR(14) below current price.
Do NOT tighten the stop before the 2R partial. Premature tightening is the most common cause of being stopped out on trades that would have won.
If price returns to entry after the 2R partial: the remaining 50% closes at breakeven. Net result = winner on the first half, breakeven on the second. Still a positive outcome.

4.4 Timeout Management

Timeout ScenarioAction
96 bars (48h) elapsed — position is profitableClose at market. Lock in the gain. Capital is needed for new signals.
96 bars elapsed — position near entry (within 0.5R)Close at market. The signal has exhausted its momentum. Do not extend the hold.
96 bars elapsed — 2R partial already fired, stop at breakevenHold. Effectively risk-free. Allow the remaining 50% to run to TP target.
96 bars elapsed — position significantly negative, approaching stopLet the stop execute. Do not close manually above the stop just because the timeout clock arrived.

Risk Controls

These limits are hard constraints. They override any signal quality assessment, grade, or market opinion. The asymmetry of drawdown recovery (a 20% loss requires a 25% gain to recover) demands systematic protection.
